Too much CO2 results in excessive global warming and ocean acidification. Human civilization developed under an atmosphere with relatively low CO2 concentrations of ~300 ppm. During the last ice age CO2 was only ~180 ppm and this made Earth colder and glaciers covered much of Northern Europe and North America. These low CO2 concentrations made it more difficult for plants to grow and human populations diminished during ice ages. In contrast, atmospheric CO2 much greater than 300 ppm causes many problems for society, including global warming, rising seas levels, more extreme weather events (floods and droughts), expansion of tropical diseases to regions that currently do not have them, and acidification of the oceans. For our society, around 300-350 ppm CO2 is good and much more or much less is bad.
