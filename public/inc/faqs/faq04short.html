In addition to CO2, other greenhouse gases include water vapor (H2O), methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O). The amount of water vapor in the atmosphere increases in a warmer world and therefore responds to climate changes controlled by other greenhouse gases. Scientists currently only have paleo-proxies for CO2 and we know very little about other greenhouse gases beyond the past 800,000 years that have been studied from ancient air bubbles extracted from ice cores.
