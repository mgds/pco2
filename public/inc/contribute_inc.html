Many studies only report their data graphically and do not include tables of raw data or information necessary to fully replicate their results. As proxies evolve and original authors cannot always be contacted, it is important to report all details of a given reconstruction, including sampling specifications, all raw data, constants and calibrations used to translate proxy data to CO2 and its propagated 95% confidence limits. The following templates have been designed by proxy experts to secure the longevity of each data set, allow future addition of new data constraints, and update calculations when proxy understanding evolves.

How to Contribute Data

  • Choose your template from the proxy templates provided.
  • Enter your data in the same format as the example data in the first rows.
    • Report data in the same units as the template, with fully propagated 2σ uncertainties
    • Enter "NA" where specification is not applicable.
    • The level of detail in these templates is high but important, and reporting consistent error propagation is crucial for ascertaining comparability between different CO2 estimates and for creating a reliable paleo-CO2 compilation.
    • Add new columns after the last column of the template if it does not yet specify an important parameter of your study.
    • When you have entered all data, delete the example data
  • Save your spreadsheet with the first author's last name and publication year in the file name (e.g. paleosol_breecker_2020.xlsx)
  • Send the completed spreadsheet to info@paleo-co2.org to check all entries for consistency. paleo-co2.org and authors will then jointly submit the spreadsheets to the Paleo-CO2 Database at the NOAA National Centers for Environmental Information (NCEI, formerly NCDC), and the information will be displayed on this website.
For any questions or concerns, please contact info@paleo-co2.org.
